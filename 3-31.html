Picnic

ピックニック

March 31, 2018
Another full day without any obligations whatsoever - For meals, I raided the convenience store (again). I suspect I'll be tired of the food by the end of the program, but right now I'm completely satisfied. There's a baseball field with a row of cherry trees nearby - a nice spot we chose for a spontaneous spring picnic.

It's a chilly day, and I know I didn't pack enough warm clothes. I only thought of sweaty summers, not a spring colder than San Diego's winter. What helps is that convenience stores are not only stocked with cold drinks, there's also a section for heated coffees, milk teas, and... other things.

Unfortunately none of my selections were very good, but the thrill of trying something new is worth it. And there is always something new to try.

Hot lemon - this is a sickly sweet cross between gatorade and lemonade, not the honey lemon tea I was hoping for!

Ume Musubi - Sushi rice with sour plum - 120 yen (~1 USD)

Greasy noodles but with lots of veggies - 426 yen (~ 4.50 USD)

There's a small produce store owned by an old couple down the street from the nearest 7-11. It's the most chaotic, hot mess of a business I have ever seen. Half of the store contains produce and the other half looks like a junkyard with random, single pots and pans, but surprisingly neat packets of city-ordained trash bags. You have to wade through boxes of ugly potatoes and cabbages to get to the produce fridge. There's a freezer with iced over gari-gari kuns (popsicles) that looks like it's been there for years. Everything is in cardboard boxes, prices and source location labeled with a black marker. I checked my purchases thoroughly, so as to not buy something overripe or moldy.

It's a mess, but it's cheap, so I actually find everything endearing rather than a headache.

A walk through a different part of the neighborhood revealed another shrine, tucked charmingly in a corner.

My throat feels scratchy and I feel signs of an impending cold, so it's good to have a relaxing day to rest.
